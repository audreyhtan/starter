Sochi 2014

A mobile app for the Winter Olympics in Sochi, Russia

I nspired to create something new and try my hand at mobile design, I created these mockups for an app that would consolidate the news, events schedules and athlete profiles of the 2014 Winter Olympics in Sochi. The number of sporting events, sub-categories per event and daily schedules made this a fun information architecture challenge. Being limited to tap and swipe motions was good practice in articulating detailed interaction gestures unique to mobile devices.

Waggit

Your neighborhood dog sitting exchange

A fter five years of IT consulting, Waggit was my brainchild that got me hooked on entreprenuership and plugged into the New York startup community. A website for dog owners to connect and help each other with dog sitting and dog walking, it was my first attempt at creating something from the ground up. I formed my own company and spent two years developing the product, overseeing design and development, doing marketing and building strategic partnerships. Gearlance

P2P rental marketplace for photography and video gear

P art of my journey as a web designer was doing a 3-month program called The Starter League in Chicago, IL. During the program, I learned advanced HTML and CSS techniques and used preprocessors like HAML and SASS in a Ruby on Rails environment. As a final project, I joined a team of developers to create Gearlance, a marketplace where people rent camera equipment directly from their peers, save money and be part of a community. The goal was to build a platform that could be white-labeled to support a P2P marketplace within any industry vertical. Built on Twitter Bootstrap, I crafted the user experience, design aesthetic and did front end development.
